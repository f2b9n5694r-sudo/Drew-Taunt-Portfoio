Student research involvement

I am part of the Torres Dowdall Lab at Notre Dame. The lab studies the causes and consequences of adaptive evolution, especially how phenotypic variation changes across ecological gradients and how genetic, developmental, molecular, and environmental factors interact to produce biological diversity.

The lab’s work includes systems such as fish and traits related to morphology, life history, and visual sensitivity/color vision. My role is represented conservatively as a student lab member gaining research training and exposure to the scientific process.

Adaptive Evolution

How organisms respond to ecological pressures over time.

Phenotypic Variation

How traits vary across environments, populations, and gradients.

Scientific Training

Learning through lab culture, collaboration, data, and careful observation.
